Opportunity03
01 · Opportunity

Growth depends on enough demand for activation to convert.

Activation running consistently against a limited demand pool sees efficiency drop over time — CPA climbs, audiences tap out.

The opportunity: a deliberate investment in upper-funnel awareness that expands the pool — reaching people who aren't ready to buy today but will be in 6, 12, 24 months.

This deck is about what that investment looks like and how it pays back.

Dynamic 1 · Brand architecture04
02 · Brand architecture

Ant International and WorldFirst both invest in brand — together.

When buyers see both logos together, the signal can split — each dollar may do less for WorldFirst specifically than if it anchored a single brand.

This isn't about less investment in either. It's about having a clearer policy on when to lead with Ant (institutional legitimacy) and when to lead with WorldFirst (product, proposition, moat).

A thoughtful architecture turns the two-brand dynamic into a multiplier.

Dynamic 2 · Benchmark05

Binet & Field and the LinkedIn B2B Institute find ~46% brand / 54% activation as the long-term B2B fintech optimum mix. WorldFirst's current investment tilts more heavily toward activation — a sensible stance, CPA-driven decisions are legible to finance and easier to attribute.

How we measure32
30 · How we measure

Four categories. Four jobs.

01 · Content performance
  • Impressions
  • Time spent
  • Engagement rate
Measured weekly. The input metric.
02 · Direct acquisition
  • Website visits
  • App downloads
  • Registrations
Measured weekly. Direct conversion signal.
03 · Audience building
  • Social media audiences
  • Website audiences (retargeting pool for paid)
Measured monthly. The compounding asset.
04 · Cycle signals
  • Brand-search lift
  • Indirect conversion (3 / 6 / 12 months)
Evaluated quarterly. The long-horizon payback.
