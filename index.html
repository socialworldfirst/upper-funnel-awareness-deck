The 5 stages of awareness03
02 · Where our content actually sits

Where our content sits on Schwartz's five stages of awareness.

Eugene Schwartz, Breakthrough Advertising (1966). Every buyer moves through these five stages before purchase. Great content meets them at the stage they're in.

Upper-funnel Stage 01 Unaware Doesn't know they have a problem. e.g. general business content, brand campaigns that plant a problem.
Q2 focus Stage 02 Problem-aware Knows something is wrong. Losing money. Doesn't know solutions exist. e.g. "You're losing 3% every time you pay a Chinese supplier."
Q2 focus Stage 03 Solution-aware Knows solutions exist. Doesn't know which fits their case. e.g. "Here are the ways to pay Chinese suppliers, and what works when."
Where WF sits today Stage 04 Product-aware Knows WorldFirst. Weighing if it fits. e.g. "WorldCard gets 1% cashback. World Account holds 20 currencies."
Lower-funnel Stage 05 Most aware Knows WorldFirst is right. Ready to sign up. e.g. pricing, CTA ads, retargeting, sign-up flow.

WorldFirst dynamics5
04 · What's specific to WorldFirst

Three dynamics shape our demand-building specifically.

  • Brand architecture — Ant and WorldFirst show up together. Each dollar can do less for WorldFirst specifically unless we have a clear policy on when each brand leads.
  • Regional reality — different markets, different messages. SEA isn't Europe. Vietnam isn't the US. Regional intelligence has to be baked into the design.

Why 20M25
24 · The number

Why 20M? Enough reach to seed demand, not just measure it.

  • Problem-aware audiences are larger than in-market 5% — need enough reach to find them across each priority region
  • Repetition matters — one exposure plants, three exposures land, so each targeted buyer needs ~3 touches
  • 20M across Q2 / 10 priority regions / 60 days works out to the floor for reach × repetition per targeted pool
  • Below that we're producing content without enough delivery to know what actually lands
This isn't a brand-arithmetic target. It's the delivery floor for testable demand-building.

Brand campaign is risky37
35 · Brand campaigns come with real risk

Two reminders of what goes wrong when brand outpaces defence.

  • Wise, 2014–19 — built the "hidden fees" category for the whole market. Much of the demand got collected by Revolut, Remitly and Atlantic Money. Brand without activation defence subsidised competitors.
  • WorldFirst × Spurs — the football sponsorship delivered visibility but hasn't translated into upper-funnel awareness our activation teams can compound. A brand beat without the content layers to carry it through.
Brand is high-leverage. High-leverage means it cuts both ways.

Brand campaigns should create awareness that's ours to collect, not competitors'.
